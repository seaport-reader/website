Overview

This project was part of a large, multi-year public health data modernization initiative focused on improving how health data is ingested, processed, and used during infectious disease response. The initiative was launched in response to major gaps exposed during the COVID-19 pandemic, when public health departments struggled to handle the volume and complexity of incoming data from healthcare providers.

Our team worked on a product aimed at improving the usability of electronic case reports (eCRs)—a relatively new data standard intended to automate and streamline disease reporting from healthcare systems to public health agencies.

Context

Electronic case reporting (eCR) enables real-time, automated reporting of detailed medical record data to public health agencies. The goal of eCR is to reduce manual reporting for healthcare providers while providing public health staff with more complete and timely data to track disease spread and outbreaks.

In practice, however, adoption of eCR has been slow. Public health staff often receive eCRs that contain a patient’s entire medical record, making it difficult to quickly find the information relevant to their work. As a result, many users found eCRs frustrating or unusable and reverted to older, manual workflows.

The Challenge

After running interviews and a co-collaborative workshop with WxPO, we learned:

  1. Long history of distrust between development team and users
  2. Users had difficulty, or were reluctant to give feedback
  3. Multiple applications used to complete one task, but applications were disparate and slow
  4. Users lacked an ‘end-to-end’ view of the data
  5. They wanted to see where the data was coming in (ingest), everything that happens in between, and data going out (subscriptions).

Research & Discovery

We began with comprehensive user research to understand pain points and opportunities. This included user interviews, usability testing of the existing platform, competitor analysis, and analytics review.

Trust Issues

2

Information Overload

3

Mobile Frustration

Mobile users struggled with small touch targets and slow load times

4

Design Solution

Based on our research, we developed a comprehensive redesign strategy focused on simplification, trust-building, and mobile optimization.

Streamlined Checkout

Reduced checkout from 6 steps to 3, with guest checkout option and progress indicators. Displayed shipping costs upfront to eliminate surprises.

Enhanced Product Pages

Redesigned product pages with high-quality images, clear CTAs, and simplified information hierarchy. Added user reviews prominently.

Smart Search & Filters

Implemented intelligent search with autocomplete and visual filters that helped users find products 3x faster.

Mobile-First Design

Created a responsive design with thumb-friendly navigation, optimized images, and faster load times.

Trust Signals

Added security badges, clear return policy, and customer testimonials throughout the experience.

Personalization

Integrated recommendation engine that showed relevant products based on browsing history and preferences.

Key Takeaways

  • User research is invaluable - the insights we gained directly informed our most successful design decisions
  • Simplification can be more challenging than adding features, but it's often what users need most
  • Mobile-first thinking improved both mobile and desktop experiences
  • Small trust signals (security badges, reviews, clear policies) have significant impact on conversion
  • Iterative testing and refinement after launch continued to improve metrics
